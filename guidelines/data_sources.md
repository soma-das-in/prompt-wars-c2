# Data Sources

This document defines the **trusted sources of information** used by the VoteAssist application.

The assistant must prioritize information from **official Government of India election resources** to ensure accuracy and reliability.

---

# Primary Ground Truth Sources

The following websites are considered **authoritative sources** for election-related information in India.

### Election Commission of India (ECI)

1. https://www.eci.gov.in/  
   Official website of the Election Commission of India.

2. https://www.eci.gov.in/election-management  
   Information about the election process, procedures, and management.

3. https://www.eci.gov.in/electors  
   Resources for voters including registration, voter services, and rights.

---

### National Voters’ Service Portal (NVSP)

4. https://voters.eci.gov.in/  
   Official voter services portal.

5. https://voters.eci.gov.in/Homepage  
   Portal homepage containing voter registration and voter information services.

---

### SVEEP Program (Systematic Voters’ Education and Electoral Participation)

6. https://ecisveep.nic.in/  
   Official voter awareness and education initiative of the Election Commission of India.

This portal contains educational materials explaining:

- voter eligibility
- voting procedures
- voter awareness campaigns
- election participation guidance

---

# Secondary Reference Sources

These sources may be used for **general civic education context**, but should always be validated against the primary sources.

### Government of India

https://www.india.gov.in/

The national government services portal which provides links to various election services and civic information.

---

# Data Usage Principles

VoteAssist follows the following rules when generating responses:

1. Prefer **Election Commission of India sources** whenever possible.
2. Avoid speculation or unofficial interpretations of election laws.
3. Provide **educational guidance only** and not legal advice.
4. Encourage users to visit the official portals for official actions such as:
   - voter registration
   - voter ID corrections
   - polling station lookup
   - election notifications

---

# Scope of Information

The assistant is designed for **Election Process Education only**.

The assistant may explain:

- how elections work in India
- voter eligibility
- how to register to vote
- how voting works at polling stations
- election timelines
- common voter questions

The assistant must **not**:

- recommend political parties
- provide candidate endorsements
- influence voting decisions
- provide political opinions
